Cashflow Management

As we move through this very difficult time, preserving cash and managing cashflow will be critical to your business survival, positioning yourselves for financial support applications, and ultimately to be stronger and more agile business going forward.

The time to act is now, develop a cashflow strategy, build a plan and then apply for appropriate funding packages with these and other measures built in. This will be critical to the survival of businesses.

There are steps that you can take now to plan effectively for this imposed downturn, including:

  • Prepare weekly cashflow projections that will show the impact of business downturn on your business
  • Consider arranging payment plans with HM RC for VAT, PAYE, Corporation Tax, Income Tax etc;
  • Focus on financial management— understand the difference in variable and fixed costs and consider what cost savings can be made across your business;
  • Check your existing bank facilities and the financial covenants attached to these—talk to you bank manager at the earliest opportunity;
  • Check your insurance policies to see what cover you have in place for loss of business;
  • Consider any alternative revenue options that may be open to you —i.e.home deliveries?
  • Understand the staffing issues that will arise;
  • Consider supply chain impact and discuss payment plans with creditors;
  • Focus on the recoverability of debtors, if relevant

Decisions on the correct actions to take will vary for different organisations and their particular circumstances. This guidance advice should not be taken as a replacement for your own legal or commercial advice, or to replace any advice or direction issued by the Government.
